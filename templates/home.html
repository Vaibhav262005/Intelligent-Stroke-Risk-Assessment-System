Understanding Brain Stroke

What is a Brain Stroke?

A brain stroke, or cerebrovascular accident (CVA), occurs when blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die within minutes.

There are two main types of strokes:

  • Ischemic stroke: Caused by a blockage in an artery that supplies blood to the brain
  • Hemorrhagic stroke: Caused by bleeding in or around the brain

A "mini-stroke" or transient ischemic attack (TIA) is a temporary disruption of blood flow to the brain that doesn't cause permanent damage but serves as a warning sign.

Risk Factors

Controllable Risk Factors
  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • High cholesterol
  • Physical inactivity
  • Obesity
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
Uncontrollable Risk Factors
  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Gender (stroke is more common in men, but women are more likely to die from stroke)
  • Family history
  • Race (higher risk in Asians, Hispanics, and African-Americans)
  • Prior stroke or TIA

Prevention Methods

  • Control blood pressure: Regular monitoring and medication if necessary
  • Manage diabetes: Check blood sugar regularly and follow treatment plans
  • Lower cholesterol: Healthy diet, exercise, and medication if necessary
  • Stop smoking: Smokers have 2-4 times higher risk of stroke than non-smokers
  • Maintain healthy weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is ideal
  • Exercise regularly: At least 30 minutes of moderate activity most days
  • Eat a healthy diet: Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low in saturated fats
  • Limit alcohol: No more than 1-2 drinks per day
  • Regular check-ups: Follow your doctor's recommendations for health screenings
Up to 80% of strokes are preventable through lifestyle changes and addressing risk factors.

Recognize the Signs (FAST)

F

Face Drooping

One side of the face droops or is numb

A

Arm Weakness

One arm is weak or numb

S

Speech

Speech is slurred or difficult

T

Time to Call

Call emergency services immediately

Every minute counts! Prompt treatment can minimize brain damage and potential complications.
